## How Google Flow pricing works in India

### Subscription plans (monthly, includes Flow credits)

| Plan | Price (INR/mo) | Flow credits/month | Effective INR per credit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Free | ₹0 | 50/day (trial only) | not viable for Filmathon |
| Google AI Plus | ₹399 | 200 | ~₹2.00 |
| Google AI Pro | ₹1,950 | 1,000 | ~₹1.95 |
| Google AI Ultra | ₹6,500 | 10,000 | ~₹0.65 |

Credits do not roll over month to month.

### Cost per video generation (Veo 3.1 in Flow)

| Model tier | Credits per generation |
|---|---:|
| Lite | 10 |
| Fast | 20 |
| Quality | 100 |

One "generation" can produce multiple clips depending on the feature. Students will iterate, so real usage is higher than a single pass.

### Top-up credits (Pro / Ultra only)

Google sells one-time AI credit packs (e.g. 2,500 / 5,000 / 20,000). Exact INR pricing is shown at purchase time in Google One. Budget using the Pro-plan effective rate (~₹1.95/credit) until WIF gets a live quote.

## What one short AI film actually costs

Assumption: 60-120 second short, fully AI-generated in Flow, mostly Lite/Fast with some Quality hero shots.

| Usage pattern | Generations | Credits needed | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Conservative | 25 Lite + 10 Fast | 450 | Tight, little room for iteration |
| Realistic | 20 Fast + 15 Lite + 3 Quality | 700 | Normal student iteration |
| Heavy | 30 Fast + 10 Quality | 1,600 | Ambitious visual film |

**Planning number per selected filmmaker: 800-1,000 credits.**

That is roughly one Google AI Pro month (1,000 credits), but many students will burn through it with retries. Safer to budget **1,200-1,500 credits per filmmaker** or Pro + a small top-up.

## Process recommendation for India

This keeps judging clean and limits credit spend.

1. **Announce a single national topic** after each Flow workshop event (theme must be about AI, positive portrayal of AI - per Google).
2. **Open script submission** to anyone interested (not only room attendees).
3. **WIF curates scripts** down to a shortlist (e.g. 100 -> 50).
4. **WIF selects final filmmakers** (e.g. 50 -> 30) based on script strength and feasibility in Flow.
5. **Issue Flow access** via Pro accounts or WIF-managed credit pool for a fixed window (7-14 days recommended, not 48 hours).
6. **Films must be:**
   - fully AI-generated in Google Flow / Google AI tools
   - no live-action footage
   - on-brief (AI theme, positive framing)
7. **Jury screens submissions** -> WIF shortlist -> Mumbai awards (1st, 2nd, 3rd).

48 hours is likely too short for meaningful Flow films once WIF is paying per credit. **7-14 days** is more realistic and still feels like a Filmathon.
