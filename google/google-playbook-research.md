# Google precedents to borrow from

## Short answer

Google has not publicly run this exact WIF India format, but it has already done the key pieces:

- filmmaker cohorts with unlimited Flow access, mentorship, workshops, and short films
- AI filmmaking education with Sundance Institute
- India-scale AI skilling for creators with Google + YouTube + IICT
- showcase infrastructure through Flow TV and YouTube

The WIF proposal should be framed as an India execution of an existing Google playbook, not a totally new experiment.

## 1. Flow Sessions

Source: Google Labs, Flow Sessions.

What Google did:

- selected artists / filmmakers
- gave them unlimited access to Flow
- provided mentorship and AI education
- supported them while they created short films over roughly two months
- published learnings around director's mindset, experimentation, and personal storytelling

Why it matters for WIF:

This is the closest model. WIF should ask Prashant:

> Can we adapt the Flow Sessions model for India, with WIF as the community and execution partner?

Useful elements to borrow:

- selected cohort, not everyone
- credits/access for a defined creation window
- mentorship + workshops
- final short film output
- public learnings / showcase

Sources:

- https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/google-labs/flow-resident-filmmaker/
- https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/google-labs/3-things-learned-flow-sessions/

## 2. Google.org + Sundance Institute

Source: Google.org announcement, Jan 2026.

What Google did:

- $2M funding to Sundance Institute
- AI education for filmmakers
- community-led ecosystem with Sundance, The Gotham, Film Independent
- free online curriculum
- AI Creators Fellowship
- community conversations, case studies, and industry standards

Why it matters for WIF:

This proves Google already believes AI film education should be community-led and run through trusted film institutions. WIF is the India equivalent partner for women-led and representation-forward film communities.

Useful elements to borrow:

- trusted film organization as delivery partner
- education + ethics + standards
- fellowship / creator cohort
- case studies as an output

Source:

- https://blog.google/company-news/outreach-and-initiatives/google-org/sundance-institute-ai-education/

## 3. AI Skills House x MIB x IICT x Google + YouTube

Source: AI Skills House.

What Google is doing in India:

- national AI skilling initiative with Ministry of Information & Broadcasting and IICT
- targets students, creators, creative professionals, developers
- free access to Google AI courses
- two-phase structure:
  - Phase 1: online foundation
  - Phase 2: blended online/in-person, project-based creative specialization
- includes tools like Gemini, Veo, AI Studio, Vertex AI
- includes storytelling and YouTube best practices

Why it matters for WIF:

This is the India-scale precedent. WIF should not duplicate it; WIF should become the premium film-industry activation layer for it.

Useful positioning:

> AI Skills House is the broad national skilling layer. WIF x Google AI Creator Lab is the high-touch film-industry activation layer across top film hubs.

Source:

- https://aiskillhouse.iict.org/

## 4. Flow TV

Source: Flow TV / Flow launch.

What Google built:

- public showcase for clips, channels, and short films made with Flow / Veo
- viewers can see prompts and techniques behind clips
- works as both showcase and learning library

Why it matters for WIF:

The student AI Filmathon should not end in a vague "showcase." It should produce a Flow TV / YouTube-ready showcase format:

- top films
- prompts / process notes
- creator interviews
- before / after pipeline assets

Sources:

- https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/google-flow-veo-ai-filmmaking-tool/
- https://labs.google/flow/tv/channels

## Recommended playbook for WIF

Use a combination of Flow Sessions + AI Skills House:

1. **Student workshops:** 2-day campus workshops at film schools / universities, livestreamed where possible.
2. **Professional masterclasses:** 2-4 hour sessions at industry venues for working filmmakers and departments.
3. **Selected cohorts:** Not everyone gets credits. Select 20-50 students per student venue.
4. **Credit window:** Give selected students program-level Flow / Veo / Gemini credits for a fixed window.
5. **WIF x Google AI Filmathon:** selected filmmakers create short AI films in Google Flow using WIF-funded credits (Google credits out of scope post-Prashant call).
6. **Mentorship:** Light-touch office hours, not a full course.
7. **Mumbai awards / PR event:** WIF-selected films are screened with Google, WIF, a jury, and industry leaders; Top 3 films are awarded.
8. **Showcase:** Top work goes on YouTube / Flow TV-style page, with prompts and process notes.
9. **Case studies:** Publish 5-10 creator stories as Google marketing assets.
