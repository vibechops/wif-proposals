# What to ask Google: Option A vs Option B pricing

Deep-research backing for the WIF India program fee. FX used: $1 ≈ ₹85.

> **Decision (internal call, Jun 2026):** The tiered approach was adopted. Final asks in the proposal are **Option A USD $100,000** and **Option B USD $150,000** (B set at the stretch anchor on Ronak's recommendation). Program length also extended to 16 weeks. The numbers below are the original research backing.

## TL;DR recommendation

| | Option A (events only) | Option B (events + Filmathon + awards) |
|---|---:|---:|
| **Recommended ask** | **USD $60,000** (~₹51L) | **USD $120,000** (~₹1.02 cr) |
| Negotiation floor | $45,000 (~₹38L) | $95,000 (~₹81L) |
| Stretch / open-high anchor | $75,000 | $150,000 |

Why split the fee: the current proposal quotes a **flat $100,000 for either option**. That is strategically weak. If Google picks A (less work) WIF is overpaid and looks padded; if Google picks B (roughly 2x the work plus WIF-funded Flow credits and a Mumbai awards show) WIF is underpaid and may lose margin. A tiered price makes B the obvious better-value choice and protects WIF either way.

The $100K already floated to Prashant becomes the **B-tier negotiation floor**, not the headline. A is introduced as a cheaper entry point.

## How the numbers are built (cost-plus + market sanity check)

### Option A: 7-city college roadshow, 16 weeks

WIF delivers venues, event production, audience, on-ground management, and a report/content package. Google supplies the Flow expert.

| Cost line | Low | Base | High |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 7-city event production (₹1–2L/city) | ₹7L | ₹10.5L | ₹14L |
| Travel + logistics (expert + WIF crew) | ₹1.5L | ₹2.5L | ₹4L |
| Marketing / comms (3,500 member base + social) | ₹1L | ₹1.5L | ₹2L |
| Program management (3 months) | ₹2L | ₹3L | ₹4L |
| Report + content package to Google | ₹0.5L | ₹0.75L | ₹1L |
| **WIF cost** | **₹12L** | **₹18.25L** | **₹25L** |
| **≈ USD** | **~$14K** | **~$21K** | **~$29K** |

**Recommended A ask: $60K (~₹51L).** That is a healthy but normal program margin on cost, and it sits at the low-mid end of the India activation market (see below), which is the right place for a partner that brings its own audience and credibility rather than a pure vendor.

### Option B: everything in A + script call + Filmathon + Mumbai awards

| Added cost line (on top of A) | Low | Base | High |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Module 2: national script call + curation | ₹0.5L | ₹1L | ₹2L |
| Module 3: Flow credits, 50–140 selected filmmakers (~₹3K each) | ₹1.5L | ₹2.5L | ₹4.2L |
| Module 4: Mumbai awards / PR event | ₹3L | ₹5L | ₹8L |
| Extra PM + marketing for the back half | ₹2L | ₹3L | ₹4L |
| **Added WIF cost** | **₹7L** | **₹11.5L** | **₹18.2L** |
| **Option B total WIF cost (A + added)** | **₹19L** | **₹29.75L** | **₹43.2L** |
| **≈ USD** | **~$22K** | **~$35K** | **~$51K** |

Prize money for 1st/2nd/3rd is extra and should be carved out or co-funded, not buried in the fee.

**Recommended B ask: $120K (~₹1.02 cr).** Covers the larger delivery plus a fair program margin, and is consistent with how national multi-city activations *with a marquee awards finale* are priced in India.

These are what the program would cost if Google hired a commercial agency to run it. They justify the ask as market-rate, not inflated.

## Google's own comparable collaborations (the "what does Google actually pay" question)

| Program | What Google gave | Structure | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google.org × Sundance Institute** (Jan 2026) | **$2,000,000** over 3 years | Philanthropic grant (AI Opportunity Fund), trains 100,000+ filmmakers, free curriculum + AI Creators Fellowship + ethics alliance | Closest precedent. Proves Google funds *trusted film orgs* to run community-led AI film education. WIF = the India equivalent. $2M dwarfs a $60–120K ask. |
| **YouTube India creator investment** | **₹850 crore** over 2 years | Ecosystem investment in Indian creators/media | Shows Google/YouTube spends at massive scale on Indian creators. Context that 6-figure USD is rounding-error. |
| **AI Skills House (MIB × IICT × Google × YouTube)**, May 2026 | Undisclosed; 15,000 scholarships, free Google AI courses | Govt-anchored national skilling, 2-phase | The broad national layer. WIF should position as the *premium film-industry activation layer* on top, not a duplicate. |
| **Flow Sessions** | In-kind: unlimited Flow access + mentorship + workshops, cohort of selected artists | No cash to artists; Google-run pilot | Source of the format. Note: Google gave *access, not cash* here, which is why Prashant said credits are out of scope. WIF's cash ask is for *execution*, a different bucket. |
| **Filmmaker-in-residence (Henry Daubrez)** | Salaried hire + content | Internal | Shows Google pays for creative talent/output directly. |

### The key framing nuance

Two different Google money buckets are in play:

1. **Philanthropy (Google.org / AI Opportunity Fund):** Sundance $2M lives here. Large, mission-framed grants.
2. **Product/marketing activation (Google AI / Prashant's side):** This is where the WIF fee will most likely come from. These budgets pay market activation rates, which is exactly why the India activation benchmarks above are the right comparison set.

So pitch the *value* using Sundance/YouTube scale ("Google already invests millions in this exact space"), but *justify the specific number* using the India activation market rates. The ask is small relative to the philanthropy precedent and fair relative to the activation market. Both framings push the same direction: $60K / $120K is reasonable-to-conservative.

## How to present it to Google

1. **Lead with value, not cost.** "Google already committed $2M to Sundance for AI film education and ₹850 cr to Indian creators. This is the India film-school activation layer for Flow, for a fraction of that."
2. **Show the ladder.** A at $60K is the entry; B at $120K is ~2x the work for 2x the fee, plus the Mumbai awards moment and a library of creator films/case studies Google can use as marketing assets.
3. **Carve out prizes and any Flow credit top-ups** as separate lines so the core fee stays clean.
4. **Hold the floors quietly:** don't go below $45K (A) or $95K (B). If Google only has the previously-discussed $100K, steer them to **B at $100K** (still above WIF cost) rather than discounting A.
