# What to ask Dell: sponsorship tier pricing

Research backing for WIF India x Dell sponsorship fees. FX: $1 ≈ ₹85.

## TL;DR recommendation

| | Tier 1 (Lab) | Tier 2 (Lab + Exhibition) | Tier 3 (All 3, Y1) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Recommended ask** | **USD $150,000** | **USD $265,000** | **USD $595,000** |
| Negotiation floor | $125,000 | $230,000 | $520,000 |
| Stretch anchor | $175,000 | $300,000 | $650,000 |

**Lead with Tier 2.** It gives Dell the strongest visibility story (writers developed + films on screens) without the $595K Year 1 commitment of Tier 3. Tier 1 works as a foot-in-the-door if budget is tight.

## How the numbers are built

### Cost-plus from intake documents

| Program | Source | Internal cost (INR) | USD at ₹85/$ |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Screenwriters Lab (3-day) | Budget PDF | ₹1.10 Cr | ~$130K |
| Exhibition (draft) | event-spec.md | ₹75 L | ~$88K |
| Internship (annual) | Kamla Mills doc | ₹2.5 Cr | ~$294K |

Recommended asks add margin for WIF delivery risk, tax, and program management overhead:

- Tier 1: ~15% above cost ($150K vs $130K cost)
- Tier 2: ~21% above combined cost ($265K vs $218K cost)
- Tier 3: ~16% above combined cost ($595K vs $513K cost)

---

## Why tier by program count (not flat fee)

A flat fee for "WIF partnership" is strategically weak:

- If Dell picks Lab only, a high flat fee looks padded
- If Dell picks all three, a low flat fee underprices ~₹4.4 Cr of delivery
- Tiering makes Tier 2 the obvious value step (add Exhibition for +$115K)
- Tier 3 is the flagship narrative: development + distribution + employment

This mirrors the Google proposal's Option A / B split, adapted for three parallel programs.

## Negotiation strategy

1. **Open with Tier 2 at $265K** as the recommended partnership level
2. **Tier 1 at $150K** as the fallback if Dell budget is constrained this cycle
3. **Tier 3 at $595K** as the anchor; position multi-year ($1.485M over 3 years) for full 100-internship commitment
4. **Do not go below floor** without reducing scope (fewer films, smaller intern cohort, shorter lab)
5. **In-film branding** (Sikhya / Raonaq) is a separate conversation; don't bundle into WIF program fee
